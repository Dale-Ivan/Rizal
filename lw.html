Europe: Rizal traveled to Europe in 1882 to pursue higher education. He first studied in Madrid, Spain, where he enrolled at the Universidad Central de Madrid to study medicine and the humanities. During his time in Spain, he became actively involved in the Filipino expatriate community and engaged in intellectual and political discussions.

Germany: Rizal also spent time in Germany, particularly in Heidelberg and Wilhemsfeld, where he continued his studies in ophthalmology. It was during his time in Germany that he completed his novel "Noli Me Tangere" and continued his involvement in reformist activities.
Mi Último Adiós (My Last Farewell): Written on the eve of his execution in 1896, "Mi Último Adiós" is Rizal's farewell poem to his countrymen. It expresses his love for the Philippines and his willingness to sacrifice his life for the cause of freedom and independence. The poem serves as a poignant reminder of Rizal's dedication to the Filipino people and his enduring legacy as a national hero.
Essays and Articles: Rizal was also a prolific essayist and journalist, writing numerous articles and essays on a wide range of topics, including politics, education, religion, and culture. His essays, such as "The Philippines A Century Hence" and "The Indolence of the Filipino," provide insightful critiques of Philippine society and colonial rule, advocating for social and political reforms.

Poems: Rizal composed various poems throughout his life, expressing his thoughts and emotions on love, patriotism, and social justice. His poetry reflects his deep love for his country and his desire for its liberation from colonial oppression. Some of his notable poems include "To the Philippine Youth," "Hymn to Talisay," and "To My Muse."
